Ozone Invaders Scratch Game

Scratch Game

Developed a captivating video game using Scratch to educate elementary students on the significance of the ozone layer while introducing key computer science principles.

Overview

I worked for SJSU College Corps where I contributed to the education of elementary grade students in under-served communities by teaching computer science concepts through an after school program.

One of our main tasks is to create comprehensive program kits that enhance learning concepts by developing Scratch programs and curating relevant materials.

I collaborated with a team to create Ozone Invaders, a video game to teach kids about the ozone layer and the concept of variables.


My Role

Date

  1. Cirriculum Development
  2. PowerPoint Development
  3. Programmer
  • January 2023 - April 2023

Game Concept

Objective: Protect our earth from the Sun's harmful rays by blocking the UV rays that are about to be absorbed.


The youth used variables that play a part in affecting the environment and ozone layer. The goal is to protect the earth from the harmful UV rays and minimize its effects on our environment.

Kit Development Breakdown

    1. Brainstormed a lesson topic and devised a plan to introduce a computer science concept to students.

    2. Constructed engaging PowerPoint presentations with clear agendas and goals, introducing subjects effectively, and using interactive class activities to explore concepts.

    3. Developed an engaging game using Scratchthat captivates and motivates the youth to actively participate in coding and complete the necessary steps for the game to function.

    4. Created step-by-step directions for students to follow, enabling them to learn and construct the code.

What I Learned

Through this project, I learned the importance of teamwork in achieving project success. Moreover, it served as a practical learning experience that not only familiarized me with game design, but also equipped me with the essential skills to implement code using Scratch.
